# Residential Lease Agreement

This Residential Lease Agreement ("Lease") is entered into on [Date] by and between:

- Landlord: [Landlord Name] ("Landlord"), mailing address: [Landlord Mailing Address].
- Tenants: [Tenant Name(s)] ("Tenants").

Collectively, Landlord and Tenants may be referred to as the "Parties."

## 1. Premises
Landlord leases to Tenants the residential property located at:
22723 John Rolfe Ln, Katy, TX 77449 ("Premises").

## 2. Term
The Lease begins on [Start Date] and ends on [End Date] for a fixed term of one (1) year.

## 3. Rent
Tenants agree to pay Landlord rent in the amount of [Monthly Rent] per month, due on the first day of each month. Payments shall be made to Landlord at [Payment Address] or as Landlord may otherwise direct in writing.

## 4. Security Deposit
Tenants shall pay a security deposit of [Security Deposit Amount] upon execution of this Lease. The deposit shall be held by Landlord in accordance with Texas law and may be applied to unpaid rent, damages beyond normal wear and tear, or other obligations of Tenants.

## 5. Utilities and Maintenance
- Utilities: Tenants are responsible for payment of all utilities serving the Premises.
- Yard Maintenance: Tenants are responsible for routine yard care, including mowing, watering, trimming, and general upkeep.

## 6. Use and Occupancy
The Premises shall be used solely as a private residence by the Tenants and their immediate family. No other persons may reside at the Premises without the prior written consent of Landlord.

## 7. Repairs and Alterations
Tenants shall keep the Premises clean and in good order. Tenants shall not make alterations, improvements, or additions without Landlord's prior written consent. Landlord is responsible for repairs not caused by Tenants' misuse or negligence.

## 8. Pets
[ ] Pets are not permitted.

[ ] Pets are permitted with Landlord's prior written consent and subject to additional deposit/fees.

## 9. Default and Remedies
Failure by Tenants to pay rent or perform obligations under this Lease constitutes a default. Landlord may pursue all remedies available under Texas law, including termination of the Lease and eviction.

## 10. Governing Law
This Lease shall be governed by and construed under the laws of the State of Texas.

## 11. Entire Agreement
This Lease constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties. Any modifications must be in writing and signed by both Parties.
